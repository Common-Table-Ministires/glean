# Vision

## Why this exists

Bible software nickel-and-dimes people: paywalled study notes, subscription tiers, ads inside the text itself. This app is the opposite of that on every axis. Free, forever, open source, asks nothing. If people want to give, they can, to the ministry, tax-deductible, but nothing in the app is gated behind it.

## The mechanic

Algorithmically-fed Scripture in small contextual chunks.

Not verse-of-the-day. A single isolated verse is too thin; it has no context and teaches people to treat Scripture as a fortune cookie.

Not a reading plan. Reading plans carry an implicit obligation (day 47 of 365) and most people fall behind and quit, at which point the plan itself becomes a source of guilt rather than formation.

This sits between the two: a scrollable feed of digestible passages, each with just enough surrounding context to make sense on its own without requiring the reader to have read what came before it. Read one, read twenty, close the app. No streaks, no guilt, no completion state to fall behind on.

The doomscroll form factor is deliberate. People already have the scrolling habit and the muscle memory that goes with it; that muscle memory is the delivery mechanism being borrowed. The content is the inverse of what that habit usually feeds them.

## Core principle

Formation happens through repeated small exposure over time, not through programs people graduate from. That is the thesis Common Table Ministries is built on: monastery-style formation for veterans on a regenerative farm, where the shape of the day, not a curriculum, does the forming. This app is that same thesis, unbundled from the physical site, shipped to people who will never visit Birchwood. Daily small portions. No completion. No gatekeeping.

## Ownership and distribution

Owned by Common Table Ministries, a 501(c)(3), not by any individual. This matters for three concrete reasons: donations to the project are tax-deductible through the ministry, Apple waives the developer program fee for qualifying nonprofits, and org-held copyright outlives any one person's involvement, which matters directly for the promise that this never gets sold or monetized later. See [05-apple-org-setup.md](05-apple-org-setup.md) for the mechanics.

Open source from the first commit. The license needs to specifically prevent a fork from adding ads or shipping a paid version; see [06-license-choice.md](06-license-choice.md) for the tradeoffs once that doc exists.

Donations link out to the ministry's giving page. No in-app purchase, no App Store cut, no donation prompt inside the reading flow. One quiet link in Settings and on an About screen.

## What "done" looks like for v1

Someone opens the app cold, in under two seconds, with no account and no signal, and reads a passage that stands on its own. They can keep scrolling or they can close it, and the app has no opinion about which one they choose.
